3D-Scanner Color Calibration
Goal Evaluate and improve 3D-scanner with artificial intelligence in order to optimize quality and costs for 3D-prints of miniatures.
Supported By BMWi via a ZIM-Project (alles noch verlinken
Project Partner DIG:ED
Project Start 1st of June 2019
Project End 28th of February 2021
Results The goal of this project was to optimize 3D-scanning cabines, especially to take care of that colors of the scanned person or object match the true colors. For this puprose a mapping from the scanned colors to the real colors was learned with supervised machine learning. In this way color accuracy of 3D-scans could be improved by 30% from ΔE = 5.3 to ΔE = 3.7.
Predicting the Orbifold Origin of the MSSM
Goal This work was published in the greater context of the unification of general relativity and quantum field theory. These two theories give us the best up-to-date description of nature, general relativity in the big world of things best described via gravity and quantum field theory in the tiny world of particle physics. Unfortunately this two theories contradict in some points and are not naturally complementary. String theory tries to solve this problem with a unification of both theories towards a more general one. This is the motivation and context of this project.
Published in Fortschritte der Physik / Progress of Physics
First Published 28th of April 2020
Authors Erik Parr, Patrick K.S. Vaudrevange, Martin Wimmer
Links to Paper Wiley Fortschritte der Physik / Progress of Physics: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/prop.202000032
ArXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.01732
Google Scholar https://scholar.google.de/scholar?cluster=16516052417345223115&hl=de&as_sdt=0,5
Abstract MSSM-like string models from the compactification of the heterotic string on toroidal orbifolds (of the kind 𝕋6/P) have distinct phenomenological properties, like the spectrum of vector-like exotics, the scale of supersymmetry breaking, and the existence of non-Abelian flavor symmetries. We show that these characteristics depend crucially on the choice of the underlying orbifold point group P. In detail, we use boosted decision trees to predict P from phenomenological properties of MSSM-like orbifold models. As this works astonishingly well, we can utilize machine learning to predict the orbifold origin of the MSSM.
Master's Thesis
Title Machine Learning in the Heterotic Landscape
Supervisors Prof. Andreas Weiler, Dr. Patrick Vaudrevange
Abstract This thesis has the aim to shed some light on the heterotic landscape of four-dimensional string models from orbifold compactification. Especially, we take care of MSSM-like models, which build up the relevant class of models for high energy physics. For this purpose, we use supervised and unsupervised machine learning (ML). Especially, we show with an ensamble of different ML classifiers (neural networks, gradient boosting, support vector machines, etc.) that different orbifold geometries yield fundamentally different four dimensional MSSM-like models. To visualize this effect we also make use of unsupervised ML, via a variational autoencoder. In addition, we make another prediction, to show that there are unknown relations between certain properties of four-dimensional MSSM-like models from orbifolds. These relations may build the backbone for constraints of MSSM-like models, which have their origin in string theory.
